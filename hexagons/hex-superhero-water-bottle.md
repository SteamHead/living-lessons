## Wonder Question

Would you rather have a tool that works perfectly but looks boring, or one
that looks amazing but barely works?

## Hook

Your client today does heroic things: leaps off rooftops, runs through fire,
saves cats from trees. And heroes get thirsty. Design a water bottle — or a
carrier for one — that survives your hero's life AND looks like it belongs on
a hero. Two words rule today: **functional** (how it's used) and **aesthetic**
(how it looks and makes you feel). A hero needs both.

## Mini-Lesson

Pick your hero first — invented or classic-style — and answer two questions
about them before designing: what do they DO all day (that's where function
comes from — a flying hero needs the bottle to stay put; a swimming hero
needs it to work wet), and what's their STYLE (that's aesthetics — colors,
symbols, the look that says it's theirs). Sketch with labels: every feature
gets a note saying whether it's there for function or for looks.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Design and build a water bottle (or bottle carrier) for your
superhero — useful for their heroics, styled for their identity.

**Teacher goal:** See who can say WHY each feature exists — "that's
functional, it keeps the bottle in during flips; that's aesthetic, it's her
lightning symbol" — and who designs for the hero instead of for themselves.

**Requirements:**
- Your hero named, with one sentence about what they do
- A labeled sketch before building: each feature marked F (functional) or
  A (aesthetic)
- The build shows at least two functional features and two aesthetic choices
- You can present it as the hero's gear: "this is for ___, because they ___"
- Leave the space better than you found it

**Constraints:**
- Materials from the table only
- Base material (paper or cardboard) is your teacher's call for your class
- Tool licenses apply where cutters or hot glue are used

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* a drawn hero, a built bottle shape, and ONE feature you can explain
("the strap is functional — it clips to her belt") is the full idea at work.
*Ceiling:* design for the hero's hardest moment — what does the bottle do
during the rooftop leap? — or make one feature pull double duty, functional
AND aesthetic at once, and argue why that's the best kind of design.

## Stretch Goal

Finished early? Design the same bottle for your hero's opposite — a villain,
or a hero with the reverse power — and note which features changed and which
survived. The survivors are your universal design choices.

## Badge Evidence

- *Sketching L2:* the pre-build sketch shows the design with labeled parts —
  each marked functional or aesthetic — and lists the materials it needs.
- *User Stories L1:* the hero drawing/description shows who they are, where
  their action happens, and what would go wrong with an ordinary bottle —
  a problem picture, imagined client edition.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Hold up your build. Point to one functional feature with one
  hand, one aesthetic feature with the other.
- **Tier 2:** partner-share — trade builds; the partner guesses which
  features are F and which are A before you reveal the labels.
- **Tier 3:** Photo to Google Classroom plus two sentences: your hero, and
  the one feature you'd defend if a design judge tried to remove it.
