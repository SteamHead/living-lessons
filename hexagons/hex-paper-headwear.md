## Wonder Question

Why do people wear things on their heads — and how do hats stay on?

## Hook

Sixty seconds of headwear from around the world: crowns, hard hats, chef
hats, graduation caps. Every one solves the same two problems — say something
about the wearer, and stay on. Today: stylish headwear, for yourself, paper
only. The test? It stays on while you dance.

## Mini-Lesson

Measure, wrap, fit: a paper strip around your head becomes the band everything
else builds on. Folding creates strength — same trick as the paper tower. And
sketch before you cut: a sixty-second drawing saves three sheets of paper.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Craft stylish headwear for yourself that stays on while you
dance — without using your hands.

**Teacher goal:** See who sketches before cutting, who measures to fit
instead of guessing, and who helps another maker without taking over.

**Requirements:**
- Stays on during the dance test — no hands
- A sketch made before building that shows the idea
- Two sentences: one on how the headwear represents you, one on how it is
  constructed (scribing is fine for young writers)
- Leave the makerspace better than you found it

**Constraints:**
- Paper only — no glue, tape, or any other adhesives
- Sheet limit and time set by the teacher (deck default: a few sheets, ~25 min)

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* the measured band plus one fold decoration stays on — that's a
success. *Ceiling:* design for someone else's head — a teacher's
measurements, a classmate's request. How does designing for someone else
change your choices? That question is the bridge to Empathize work.

## Badge Evidence

- *Card Craft L2:* student works with scissors, paper, and a template start
  (the band), then takes on the design challenge — the no-adhesive rule forces
  fold-and-lock joins.
- *Sketching L1:* a drawing made before building, clear enough that the
  student can explain the idea while pointing at it.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Raise your hand if your headwear survived the dance. Raise your
  hand if you borrowed an idea from someone and made it your own.
- **Tier 2:** Fashion walk — each maker shows their headwear and names one
  construction trick that made it work.
- **Tier 3:** Photo plus the two sentences (representation + construction) to
  Google Classroom.
