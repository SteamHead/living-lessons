## Wonder Question

What if you lived somewhere for days where nothing stays put — not your food,
not your toys, not even you?

## Hook

The ISS was built one piece at a time, and it can keep growing. Show a short
clip or concept image of a modular station. Today the station is getting
bigger, and each of us designs one new module to add to it. Many people from
many countries live there — not everyone speaks the same language, so lights
and signals help everyone stay safe.

## Mini-Lesson

Mid-build interruption, on the rug. Electricity needs a path. The battery has
two sides; the LED has two legs; direction matters; metal must touch metal.
Setup: one LED leg pre-taped to the battery's positive side, the other leg
bent free — tap it to the negative side and the light turns on. "This is like
closing the path." Micro-tip: tape onto tape, not straight off paper — paper
tears.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Build a paper module for our class space station. It must stand
up, and it needs one LED light that helps people do something inside.

**Teacher goal:** See who connects the LED to a *purpose* (a signal, a safety
light, a button) rather than a decoration, and who starts thinking beyond
their own module into the shared station.

**Requirements:**
- Stands upright (fold one sheet into two standing walls)
- Dock: a place to connect to another module
- Life support: food, water, and a place to sleep
- Mission: at least one activity can happen inside
- One LED with a job — you can say what the light *tells people*

**Constraints:**
- Paper, blue tape, and scissors only
- One battery, one LED per student
- Finished modules go on the black-fabric space table

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* the fold-to-stand step is demonstrated with a plain example; a
standing module with a named room type earns the build. Adults and peers
support LED troubleshooting — help received still counts. *Ceiling:* early
finishers furnish empty modules, build connectors between modules, or make
paper furniture for classmates' rooms — growing the shared station, not their
pile.

## Badge Evidence

- *Card Craft L1:* student folds and tapes paper into a standing 3D module,
  following along with the demonstrated fold — discovering what paper can do.
- *Craft Circuits L1:* student lights their LED on the coin cell (support
  allowed) and names what the light tells people — function, not decoration.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Raise your hand if you helped someone light their LED. Raise
  your hand if someone helped you.
- **Tier 2:** Station walk-through around the black table — "Who made a
  sleeping module? Who made a safety light? What rooms do we still need?"
- **Tier 3:** Teacher photographs the full station; students each record one
  sentence about their module's job (scribed for pre-writers).
