## Wonder Question

Which spot on campus is the hottest — and how would you PROVE it?

## Hook

Everyone has campus theories: the blacktop is an oven, the hallway by the
gym is the loudest place on earth, that one door slams hard enough to shake
the wall. Theories are cheap. Today you build instruments — micro:bits
reading the world with the sensors already inside them — plant them around
campus, and come back with something better than a theory: data.

## Mini-Lesson

Short, session by session:

- *Session 1 — Question & Program:* pick a campus question your board can
  answer with a BUILT-IN sensor: temperature (hottest/coldest spot?), light
  (darkest corner? when does the sun hit the garden?), accelerometer (which
  door slams hardest? does the bleacher shake?), compass, or sound on V2
  boards. Program the reading to show on the LEDs — and decide your
  threshold: what number counts as "hot," "dark," "a slam"? Test indoors,
  adjust until the numbers behave.
- *Session 2 — Deploy & Calibrate:* mount the boards on site (tape, string,
  zipper bag against weather). Then calibrate IN PLACE — the reading that
  made sense at your desk may be nonsense in the sun. Adjust, re-flash,
  re-check. Set the log routine: who visits, when, what gets written on the
  clipboard sheet.
- *Session 3 — Analyze & Exhibit:* collect boards and log sheets, chart the
  data (a hand-drawn graph is a real graph), and answer your question with
  evidence at the data exhibition.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Ask a campus question, build and deploy a sensor instrument
that gathers real data on it, and answer the question with evidence at our
data exhibition.

**Teacher goal:** See who adjusts and re-tests instead of accepting weird
readings, who keeps the log routine going without reminders, and who lets
the data change their mind.

**Requirements:**
- A written question, and the built-in sensor chosen to answer it — with why
- A working program: reading displayed, threshold chosen and defended
- Deployed and calibrated in the real location — note what you changed on
  site and why
- The log kept: at least four visits recorded on your sheet
- A chart of your data and a claim it supports, shown at the exhibition
- Boards, batteries, and mounting materials all recovered — count them out,
  count them in

**Constraints:**
- BUILT-IN sensors only — no external probes, no add-on kits
- Placements must survive weather and curious passersby: sheltered spots,
  zipper bags, and a "science in progress — please leave" label
- Get permission for any location outside our room

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* one board, one indoor location, one week of readings, one
hand-drawn chart — that's a complete instrument and a real answer.
*Ceiling:* run TWO boards on the same question in different spots and
compare (that's a real experiment), or pre-register your prediction in
writing before the data comes in and grade your past self at the exhibition.

## Stretch Goal

Finished early? Write the field guide for your instrument: a card explaining
what it measures, what the numbers mean, and its weirdest reading so far —
posted next to the deployment so any passerby can read your science.

## Badge Evidence

- *Electronic Sensors L4:* the student uses a board with a sensor input and
  an output in a guided build, then modifies it for their design — adjusting
  the threshold or logic until it reads reliably in its real location.
- *Field Research L4:* the student selects the field location themselves,
  visits it, decides what data matters, records that self-identified data,
  and explains to the team why they chose to track it.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Thumbs — did the data agree with your theory? Raise your hand
  if a reading surprised you enough to go check the sensor.
- **Tier 2:** gallery-walk of the exhibition charts — at each one, find the
  claim and ask silently: does the chart actually back it up?
- **Tier 3:** Photo of your chart to Google Classroom with one sentence:
  what would you measure next, now that you know what measuring is like?

## Re-entry Prompt

- *Session 2:* Read your question out loud at the deployment site. Is the
  board actually positioned to answer it — or just positioned where mounting
  was easy?
- *Session 3:* Before charting, read your raw log start to finish once. Circle
  the strangest number — you'll be asked about it at the exhibition.
