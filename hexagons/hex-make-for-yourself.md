## Wonder Question

You've made things for clients before. What would you make if the client
was YOU?

## Hook

Today the client is the person you know best in the whole world: you. What
bugs you? What do you wish you had? A holder for your treasures, a stand for
your water bottle, a crown for Fridays, a garage for one small car — if it
would make YOUR day better, it's a real project. Nobody knows this client
better than you do.

## Mini-Lesson

Even when you're the client, makers look before they build. Draw your
problem or wish first — you, where it happens, what's missing. Then draw
LOTS of ideas, fast: three, four, five pictures, silly ones allowed. Circle
the one that makes you happiest. THEN get materials.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Make something for yourself that you actually want — and be
able to say what it does for you.

**Teacher goal:** See who slows down to draw before grabbing materials, and
who can tell me what their build is FOR — not just what it is.

**Requirements:**
- A drawing of you and your problem or wish
- More than one idea drawn before choosing
- A build you can hold up and finish this sentence about: "This is for me
  because I ___"
- Your space left better than you found it

**Constraints:**
- Materials from the table only
- Paper is today's base material; cardboard is on the table if you want the
  harder path (cutter rules apply)

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* one drawing of your wish, two idea sketches, and a simple build
that does its one job for you — complete. *Ceiling:* build something you'll
still be using next week (test: where will it live? what might break first?),
or add one decoration that tells everyone it's YOURS without your name on it.

## Stretch Goal

Finished early? Make the tiny version: the same design, small enough to fit
in your pocket. What has to change when everything shrinks?

## Badge Evidence

- *User Stories L1:* the drawing shows the maker's own problem or wish — who
  (them), where it happens, and what's going wrong or missing.
- *Brainstorm L1:* several idea drawings made before choosing — quantity
  first, no idea wrong.
- *Card Craft L2:* a completed build using scissors, tape, and paper
  (templates welcome) — a guided start finished as the maker's own design
  choice.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Hold your build up. Finish out loud: "This is for me because
  I ___."
- **Tier 2:** partner-share — show your partner; the partner asks one
  question that starts with "why."
- **Tier 3:** Photo to Google Classroom plus one sentence: will you still be
  using this next week — why or why not?
