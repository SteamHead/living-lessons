## Wonder Question

Why do humans make things — and who are they making them for?

## Hook

Because things are useful! And today, for the first time, you have a real
client. Introduce the client list: kindergartners whose crayons roll off the
table and get lost; a teacher who starts seeds with 2nd–4th graders and needs
pots that survive being carried; our own hot glue station, which needs a
holder that keeps 4–8 glue sticks upright and obvious. Real people, real
problems. Pick yours.

## Mini-Lesson

Needs versus wants: a **need** is what the box must do or it fails (crayons
don't fall out the bottom); a **want** is what would make your client love it
(it looks nice at their table). Before touching cardboard, write or draw your
client's needs and wants in your journal. Build micro-tips: measure twice,
cut once; take cardboard from the edges of the sheet toward the center so the
scraps stay usable.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Prototype a useful box for a client of your choice.

**Teacher goal:** See how you combine your skills — measuring, cutting,
gluing — and how you practice safety without being reminded.

**Requirements:**
- Pick one client, and note their needs and wants before building
- Your box does its job: the client's objects go in, stay in, and come out
  easily
- Everyone builds their own box, even inside a tool group
- Leave the makerspace better than you found it

**Constraints:**
- Tool groups of three or fewer — one tool caddy per group
- A box template is available if you want one
- Hot glue station: four makers maximum, line up, and do NOT build at the
  station — glue and go
- Cutter and glue licenses apply

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* the template plus an adult check turns into a real, working box —
template use is still measuring, cutting, and gluing. *Ceiling:* test your
box with the client's actual objects (crayons, glue sticks, soil cup), add
one feature from the *wants* list, or make the box explain itself — a label
or icon so the client knows what it's for without asking.

## Badge Evidence

- *Card Craft L4:* student works in cardboard with hot glue and cardboard
  cutters, remembering tool safety every time, in a design of their own — the
  box goes beyond the template through choices made for their client.
- *User Stories L1:* the journal notes show the problem the client
  experiences — who they are, where it happens, and what goes wrong (crayons
  rolling away, pots falling apart) — before the build starts.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Raise your hand if your box does the job you picked it for.
  Keep it up if you tested it with real objects.
- **Tier 2:** gallery-walk — boxes on display grouped by client; leave a star
  on one *need* another maker solved well.
- **Tier 3:** Photo of your box to Google Classroom with one sentence: if
  your client could change one thing about it, what would they say?
