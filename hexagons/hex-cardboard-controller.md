## Wonder Question

Why do game controllers look the way they do? Who decided buttons belong
where your thumbs are?

## Hook

Show the weird ones: a dance pad, a guitar controller, a steering wheel. A
controller is just switches wearing a costume. Demo a janky cardboard guitar —
copper-tape leads, touchable frets, real sounds. Today you build an instrument
or controller that a **classmate** will actually play.

## Mini-Lesson

From clip to touch pad: a copper-tape lead runs from the MakeyMakey input to
wherever a finger will land; the player holds earth. Plan conductive paths so
they never cross. Micro-tips: tape onto tape, fold tape around corners instead
of cutting, and test every input *before* you decorate.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Build a cardboard controller or instrument with at least three
working inputs that a classmate can play without your help.

**Teacher goal:** See how you plan conductive paths before taping, whether you
test one input at a time, and whether the design fits the *player's* hands —
not just yours.

**Requirements:**
- Three or more working inputs using copper tape or foil
- A classmate plays it with no help from you
- You can trace and explain the circuit path for one input
- Photo or video evidence uploaded to Google Classroom
- Kits fully reassembled — count clips before returning

**Constraints:**
- Cardboard, paper, copper tape, foil, and hot glue from the station only
- One MakeyMakey per team
- Cutter and glue safety rules always apply

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* the two-pad "drum" from the printed card, upgraded with tape leads —
guaranteed first success. *Ceiling:* a playable song or game level, or a
controller sized for a K-2 buddy's hands and game — ceiling work feeds
directly into Imagination Fair.

## Badge Evidence

- *Craft Circuits L4:* student uses a kit plus cardboard, paper, and adhesives
  to enhance the form — completes the guided build, then the design challenge.
  The cardboard guitar with touchable copper-tape leads is the canonical
  picture of this level.
- *Card Craft L4:* student works in cardboard with hot glue and cardboard
  knives, remembering tool safety, in a custom design of their own.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Thumbs — did your first input work on the first try? Raise your
  hand if you found a crossed or shorted path and fixed it.
- **Tier 2:** Play-test rotation — trade controllers with another team. Name
  one thing the player did with your controller that surprised you.
- **Tier 3:** Video of a classmate playing your controller plus one sentence:
  what did you change after watching someone else use it?

## Re-entry Prompt

Session 2, on the rug: what broke overnight? Cardboard shifts and tape lifts —
check every lead before adding new ones. Decorating comes last.
