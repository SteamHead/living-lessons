## Wonder Question

What are three things you used today that needed electricity — and how did the
electricity get to them?

## Hook

Quick round robin on the rug: what IS electricity? What do we use it for?
Then hold up the copper tape: wires are roads for electricity, and this is a
flat road you can stick to paper. Today you draw the road yourself — and if
the road is complete, your LED lights at the end of it.

## Mini-Lesson

Review the rule discovered in Simple Circuits: electricity needs a complete
loop, and the LED is a one-way door — long leg, short leg, direction matters.
New micro-tips for tape roads: at corners, fold the tape around the turn
instead of cutting it (a cut can break the road); press the tape down firmly
with a fingernail; lay the LED legs flat ON the tape, metal touching metal.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Control electricity — build a paper circuit that turns your LED
on in two different ways.

**Teacher goal:** See how you use materials appropriately like an engineer,
figure things out for yourself like a coder, and help each other out like a
leader.

**Requirements:**
- Your LED turns on, two different ways (e.g., press-to-close gap, flap
  switch, or battery lift)
- Arrows drawn on your circuit showing which way the electricity flows
- You can name your components — LED, copper tape, battery — and trace the
  loop out loud
- Leave the station better than you found it

**Constraints:**
- Materials from the caddy only
- You may keep only your circuit and LED — everything else returns
- 10–15 minutes

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* a printed circuit-path template to lay tape over guarantees first
light — tracing a road still builds a real circuit. *Ceiling:* add a working
paper switch (fold-over flap), light a second LED and discover what changes,
or turn the circuit into a light-up greeting card for someone at home.

## Badge Evidence

- *Craft Circuits L2:* student builds a working copper-tape paper circuit —
  battery, tape path, LED — lights it, and can trace the complete loop, with
  arrows on the page showing which way the electricity flows. (Level per D15:
  the copper-tape paper circuit is the L2 rung between the coin-cell L1 and
  kit work at L3.)

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Raise your hand if your first road had a break in it. Keep it up
  if you found the break yourself.
- **Tier 2:** partner-share — show your partner both ways your LED turns on;
  the partner traces your loop with a finger and checks your arrows.
- **Tier 3:** Photo of your lit circuit to Google Classroom with one sentence:
  where was your hardest bug, and how did you fix it?
