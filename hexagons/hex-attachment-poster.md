## Wonder Question

How do you teach someone something when you're not in the room?

## Hook

This one is different: it's not a prototype — it's a **final product** with a
real audience. The 1st graders are starting cardboard building soon, and your
poster will hang where they work, teaching them how to connect cardboard and
paper when no big kid is around to ask. What makes a poster good enough to
teach all by itself?

## Mini-Lesson

Fast demo of three or four no-adhesive joints: the slot joint (two slits,
slide together), tab-and-slit, the folded flange (an L-shaped foot for
standing walls), and the folded hinge. The joints themselves must hold
WITHOUT glue or tape — that's the engineering your poster teaches. Then
poster craft for six-year-old readers: few words, big letters, numbered
steps, real samples they can look at up close.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** As a team, make a poster for 1st graders showing how to
connect cardboard and paper — a final product, not a prototype.

**Teacher goal:** See how you practice empathy for readers much younger than
you, how your creativity serves the client instead of yourselves, and how a
team shares one tool caddy.

**Requirements:**
- The poster is organized and clearly labeled — a 1st grader can follow it
- Real cardboard joint samples are attached, and each joint holds together
  without adhesives (mounting samples TO the poster may use glue or tape)
- Nothing falls off when the poster is held up and hung
- Show the poster to at least two students who haven't seen it, and improve
  one thing based on their questions
- Team signs the bottom-right corner

**Constraints:**
- Teams of three or fewer — one tool caddy per team
- Materials from the materials table only
- Cutter and glue licenses apply

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* one joint, well-made and labeled with a name and a number, is a real
contribution to the team poster. *Ceiling:* add a "try it" pocket holding
loose pieces so readers can practice the joint right at the poster — or
field-test with actual 1st-grade buddies and report what confused them.

## Badge Evidence

- *Workshop L3:* the team creates a documented tutorial on a detailed skill —
  the drawn-and-built poster — teaches it to at least two students, and
  improves it from their questions.
- *Setup and Cleanup L3:* the team assigns material retrieval and cleanup
  roles to each member and rotates them, so everyone learns every job.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Thumbs — could a 1st grader follow your poster without help?
  Raise your hand if a viewer's question changed your poster.
- **Tier 2:** gallery-walk of the posters — at each one, answer silently: which
  joint would YOU use first, and can you tell how from the poster alone?
- **Tier 3:** Photo of the finished poster to Google Classroom with one
  sentence: what did you change after showing it to someone?
