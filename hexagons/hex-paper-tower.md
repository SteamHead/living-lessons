## Wonder Question

Do people make things by themselves or with others?

## Hook

Show 60 seconds of famous towers (Eiffel, Burj Khalifa). What keeps a tall
thing from falling over? Then the solo warm-up: one sheet of paper each — who
can make it stand as tall as possible in 3 minutes, no other materials?

## Mini-Lesson

Folding creates strength: a flat sheet flops, a folded or rolled sheet stands.
Demonstrate one fold and one tube. That's all — the rest they discover.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Build the tallest free-standing paper tower. Your class is
competing against the other classes.

**Teacher goal:** Teamwork test — can you compete AND help each other? How the
class works together decides when we unlock more tools (hot, sharp, tiny,
messy, or valuable).

**Requirements:**
- Free-standing: cannot lean, cannot be held
- Stands on its own for 10 seconds
- Leave the makerspace better than you found it

**Constraints:**
- Paper only, no other materials, no tools
- Cannot anchor the base (no hands, tape, or glue)
- 12–15 minutes

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* the solo warm-up guarantees every student a standing structure before
teams form; one fold is a success. *Ceiling:* fast teams engineer for wind
(wave a clipboard nearby), or split a member off to coach a struggling table —
coaching feeds the tool-unlocking teacher goal.

## Badge Evidence

- *Card Craft L1:* students fold and shape paper into standing 3D forms — the
  team tower uses at least two different paper-strengthening moves (fold, tube,
  tripod, base-widening).
- *Setup and Cleanup L1:* whole-class cleanup where every student carries
  something; nobody's mess is only theirs; the room is better than we found it.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Raise your hand if you were excellent to another person today.
  Raise your hand if someone was excellent to you.
- **Tier 2:** Table walk — each team names one thing that went well and one
  thing they'd change, while the class views the tower.
- **Tier 3:** Photo of the tower to Google Classroom with one sentence: what
  would you try differently next time?
