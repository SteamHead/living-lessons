## Wonder Question

What makes hot glue grab almost anything — and what makes it let go?

## Hook

The honest confession: everyone who uses hot glue gets one tiny burn
eventually. The license isn't about never touching heat — it's about making
burns boring: tiny, rare, never big. Show the control worksheet. Glue is like
handwriting: control beats speed.

## Mini-Lesson

Safety said out loud, every time: elbow room from teammates, watch the wires,
plan the glue path, gun back in its holder when not in your hand. Then the
physics: it's hot at the nozzle AND in the fresh glue; it spreads while warm;
it grabs as it cools — hold the join until it does.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Earn your hot glue license by completing the control
worksheet — cover each printed path using only enough glue to cover the line.

**Teacher goal:** See who says the safety steps out loud before starting, who
uses the waiting list instead of hovering, and who controls the amount instead
of flooding.

**Requirements:**
- Safety recited out loud before your first squeeze
- Every worksheet path covered — with control, not puddles
- Gun returned to its holder, wires in a safe position
- Next person on the waiting list told it is their turn

**Constraints:**
- Glue station only; one gun per person at a time
- Worksheet paths only today — projects come after the license

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* straight paths with an adult nearby; a partly finished worksheet is
still real control practice. *Ceiling:* the popsicle-stick edge-move on a real
3D cardboard joint — or coach the next student through *their* safety recital.

## Badge Evidence

- *Learning Station L2:* student completes the station by following its card
  from start to finish (with a partner where helpful), and leaves the station
  ready for the next makers.
- *Setup and Cleanup L2:* student handles their own materials, returns the gun
  and wires to a safe position, and checks their area before leaving — better
  than they found it.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Thumbs — was your glue amount too much, too little, or just
  right?
- **Tier 2:** Worksheet swap — circle your partner's best-controlled path and
  tell them why you picked it.
- **Tier 3:** Photo of your worksheet plus one sentence: your personal safety
  rule, in your own words.
