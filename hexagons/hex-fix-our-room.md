## Wonder Question

What bugs you about this room that nobody has ever tried to fix?

## Hook

Every space has problems so familiar that people stop seeing them — the scrap
bin that always overflows, the caddy nobody returns right, the line-up crush
at the glue station. Today your class becomes the design firm this room hired.
And this is not pretend: there is a real budget (thank the PTA), real fixes
will be built or bought, and they will be installed HERE, where you'll live
with them every week. Choose wisely — you're your own clients.

## Mini-Lesson

One per session, short:

- *Session 1 — Observe & Define:* a ten-minute observation walk of THIS room,
  everyone with a job — tally-counter (how many people touch X), sketcher,
  measurer, question-asker (asks classmates and any staff present what slows
  them down). Pool everything on sticky notes, vote a shortlist of 3–5 real
  problems, form teams around them. Each team turns its problem into a
  driving question that names who it helps: "How might we help the class
  find returned tools faster?"
- *Session 2 — Prototype & Price:* build a cardboard/paper prototype of the
  fix — AND price the real version. What would it actually cost to buy or
  build properly? Make the item list with real prices (the posted budget is
  your reality check).
- *Session 3 — Meetings & Install:* each team gets a short meeting (see
  Challenge). Approved fixes get built-for-real or purchased, then INSTALLED
  in the room.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Find a real problem in our room, design a fix, and pitch it at
your team's meeting — aiming for your fix to be installed and used.

**Teacher goal:** See who notices problems instead of walking past them, who
prices honestly instead of wishfully, and how the room works quietly while
meetings run.

**Requirements:**
- Observation notes from Session 1 — your job's data, recorded
- A driving question that names who you're helping and what would change
- A prototype plus a priced item list for the real version
- A 3–5 minute team meeting: the problem, the fix, the cost, the ask
- If approved: install it, then check on it after a week — note one thing
  that works and one thing to improve

**Constraints:**
- The territory is THIS ROOM only — no campus-wide problems this time
- The budget is the budget (posted); over-budget fixes need the PTA path
- During meetings: the rest of the class quietly finalizes their own meeting
  work, reads, or does homework — no laptop games or videos
- Stuck for a problem? Take one from the teacher's backup list — no team
  stalls at step one

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* a team that observes, names one problem, and prototypes a simple fix
has done the full civic loop — even if it isn't chosen for install.
*Ceiling:* a fix that costs more than the class budget isn't dead — prepare
the PTA pitch: the problem, the evidence from your observation data, the
exact cost, and why it's worth it. Real board, real ask.

## Stretch Goal

Finished early? Write the maintenance card for your fix: what someone must do
weekly to keep it working, taped where the fix lives. Fixes die without
owners — give yours one.

## Badge Evidence

- *Field Research L2:* the student observes one location (our room) with
  their group and records simple data — how many people, what they use, what
  problems appear — and shares the notes with the class.
- *Driving Question L3:* the team's written question names who they are
  helping and what would change for them.
- *Service L2:* the fix is implemented in the class's immediate environment;
  the student watches how people use it and notes one thing that works and
  one thing to improve. (Granted on the install-and-check-back, so only
  installed fixes complete this badge — teams whose fix isn't chosen can
  earn it on a future run.)

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Raise your hand if your team's problem was something you'd
  walked past a hundred times without seeing it.
- **Tier 2:** partner-share across teams — trade driving questions; the
  partner says whether the question names a person and a change, or just a
  thing.
- **Tier 3:** Photo of your prototype (or installed fix) to Google Classroom
  with one sentence: what did the room teach you that you didn't notice
  before?

## Re-entry Prompt

- *Session 2:* Re-read your driving question out loud as a team. Does the
  fix you're about to build actually answer it — or did you drift toward
  something more fun to build?
- *Session 3:* Rehearse the meeting once, timed: problem, fix, cost, ask.
  Cut anything that isn't one of those four.
