## Wonder Question

If you floated while you slept, what would you find stuck to the ceiling in
the morning?

## Hook

Short ISS clip: astronauts' pencils, food packets, and tablets drifting —
and the velcro walls that catch them. You're going to the space station, and
you may bring one treasured thing. Today you make a paper version of it —
AND invent a way to wear it so it can't float away.

## Mini-Lesson

First, a one-minute draw-storm: quickly draw several things you might bring —
quantity first, no wrong ideas — then circle the one you'll make. Then the
attachment moves: loop, clip, pocket, tether. A paper necklace that holds a
paper phone; a wrist loop that grips a photo. Micro-tips: tape onto tape,
and fold-then-tape is stronger than tape alone.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Make a paper version of one thing you'd bring to the ISS, and
a wearable that keeps it attached to you in zero gravity.

**Teacher goal:** Varies by class, but centered on cooperation — watch how
tables share tape and scissors, trade attachment ideas, and whether a table
works like one crew or six strangers.

**Requirements:**
- One treasured thing, made of paper, that you can name and explain
- A wearable attachment (necklace, loop, clip, pocket) that holds it
- Passes the flip test: hold it upside down and count to five — nothing falls
- Leave the makerspace better than you found it

**Constraints:**
- Paper, tape, scissors, crayons, and string from the caddy only
- The thing and the wearable must both be yours — trading ideas encouraged,
  trading builds not

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* a paper strip necklace with the treasure taped on passes the flip
test — that's a full success. *Ceiling:* attachments that open and close
(a pocket with a fold-over flap, a clip that re-clips), a wearable for a
crewmate designed around *their* treasure, or matching gear for the whole
table crew — cooperation made visible.

## Badge Evidence

- *Card Craft L1:* student folds, tears, and tapes paper into a 3D object and
  a working wearable — discovering what paper can do before any other tools.
- *Brainstorm L1:* the draw-storm shows several drawn ideas — quantity first,
  no idea wrong — before one is chosen.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Raise your hand if you survived the flip test on the first try.
  Raise your hand if a crewmate's idea made your wearable better.
- **Tier 2:** Crew walk — each table shows their wearables together and names
  the attachment moves they used.
- **Tier 3:** Teacher photographs each wearable being worn (flip test in
  action); students each say one sentence about what their treasure is and why
  it's coming to space (scribed for pre-writers).
