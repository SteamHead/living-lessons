## Wonder Question

What is the difference between a tool and a toy?

## Hook

The honest framing, same as the hot glue license: the cutter license isn't
about never slipping — it's about making slips boring: tiny, rare, never big.
And here's the cutter's secret: **smooth is safe**. A good cut feels easy. If
you have to force it, something is wrong — and forcing is exactly when slips
happen.

## Mini-Lesson

Safety said out loud, every time: elbow's length from teammates, tuck your
thumbs, plan your carving route before the blade touches, cutter back in its
holder whenever it's not in your hand. Then the cut rule: **smooth or stop**.
If it won't cut smoothly, stop and change something — re-examine the
cardboard, review how you're holding it, or cut it another way.

## The Challenge

**Your goal:** Earn your cardboard cutter license by cutting three shapes with
control — a square for straight cuts, a circle for curvy cuts, and a star for
tight cuts.

**Teacher goal:** See who says the safety steps out loud before starting, who
stops and changes approach instead of forcing, and who uses the waiting list
instead of hovering.

**Requirements:**
- Safety recited out loud before your first cut
- All three shapes cut — smooth and controlled, thumbs tucked
- Sacrificial cardboard protecting the surface the whole time
- Cutter back in its holder, scraps in the trash, usable pieces back in the
  pile
- Next person on the waiting list told it is their turn

**Constraints:**
- Cutter station only; one cutter per person at a time
- Shapes only today — projects come after the license

## Floor & Ceiling

*Floor:* pre-drawn shape outlines to follow, with an adult nearby; a clean
square alone is real control practice. *Ceiling:* cut a shape with a window in
it (an interior cutout is the trickiest cut there is) — or coach the next
student through *their* safety recital.

## Badge Evidence

- *Learning Station L2:* student completes the station by following its card
  from start to finish (with a partner where helpful), and leaves the station
  ready for the next makers.
- *Setup and Cleanup L2:* student handles their own materials, returns the
  cutter to its holder, and checks their area before leaving — better than
  they found it.

## Reflection Menu

- **Tier 1:** Thumbs — which shape was hardest? Raise your hand if you stopped
  a cut and changed your approach instead of forcing it.
- **Tier 2:** partner-share — trade shapes with a partner; circle their
  smoothest cut edge and tell them why you picked it.
- **Tier 3:** Photo of your three shapes to Google Classroom plus one
  sentence: your personal cutter rule, in your own words.
