# Glossary

**The Living Lessons** — the projected experience a class sees together on the
classroom display: hexagon activities plus screens like the Class Journal.

**The Living Studio** — the teacher's interface, where additional detail
(skills_touched, records, editing) is viewable and editable.

**Skill / badge** — one entry in `skills/`: something a class develops, with a
five-level depth ladder. 22 core badges + 27 tool skills + 2 placeholders.

**Stage** — one of seven columns in the Design Immersion cycle: Get Inspired,
Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test, Share. Both bookends are Heart.
Defined in `skills/stages.md`.

**Pair** — a Get Inspired badge and its Share twin (inbound/outbound), e.g.
Media Explorer ↔ Digital Storytelling.

**Experience** — how many times a class has practiced a skill. Ticks on every
badge grant. Never merged with depth.

**Depth** — the highest ladder level a class has reached in a skill:
1 Introductory · 2 Early · 3 Preparatory · 4 Intermediate · 5 Advanced.
Advances when a hexagon at a higher level is completed.

**Hexagon** — a reusable classroom activity in `hexagons/`, granting 1–3
badges at stated levels. Schema in `hexagons/_README.md`.

**skills_touched** — teacher-only list of skills an activity brushes against.
Shown in the Living Studio; never on the class chart.

**Class profile** — where badges land in v1. No per-student records yet;
student profiles are a planned enhancement, and rubric language deliberately
speaks to students anyway.

**Placeholder** — Physical Prototype and Digital Prototype: chart slots that
expand into the tool wings, not badges.

**legacy_number** — a hexagon's number from the printed 2025-26 sheets;
mapping only, not an id.

**Tiers (reflection)** — 1 whole-class pulse · 2 guided share · 3 independent
evidence. Badge evidence never depends on Tier 3.

**Floor & Ceiling** — every activity's guaranteed entry point and its
extension for fast/deep finishers.

**D-numbers** — entries in `docs/decisions.md`, the project's decision log.
If it isn't in there, it isn't decided.
