# Roleplay

Act out your subjects' situations with simple props to uncover and define the problems they face.

## Depth levels

**1 — Introductory.** *(AI draft — review)* As a class, act out a short story the teacher tells about your subject's day. Freeze at the moment something goes wrong, and name the problem together.

**2 — Early.** Using simple props, roleplay experiencing a problem your subject might have — like carrying groceries with full hands. Notice what is hard, annoying, or impossible.

**3 — Preparatory.** Identify your subject's problems, and roleplay them in a group to understand the issue better. Swap roles so everyone feels the problem from a different side.

**4 — Intermediate.** Roleplay subject problems and break the play into phases. Name and discuss each phase: where does the problem start, get worse, and matter most?

**5 — Advanced.** Roleplay subject problems, record the play on video, and use the recording to write out a clear problem definition your team agrees on.
