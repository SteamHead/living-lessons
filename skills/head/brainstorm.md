# Brainstorm

Discuss and record 5-10 ideas for designs, from typical to wild.

## Depth levels

**1 — Introductory.** On your own or in loose groups, draw a multitude of pictures of things that could help your subject. Quantity first — no idea is wrong yet.

**2 — Early.** Draw lots of pictures and add text labels of anything that could help your subject, including wild, non-traditional ideas. The stranger the better at this stage.

**3 — Preparatory.** Draw and write many solutions, including outside-the-box ideas, in a group of at least three, taking turns recording. Review the final list together as a group.

**4 — Intermediate.** Brainstorm in groups of three, then combine into larger groups of six or nine to share ideas and keep building on each other's thinking.

**5 — Advanced.** Find a brainstorm process — from a book, a website, or an interview — and lead your group through it. Record the results, and reflect on whether the process helped.
