# Photo Editing

Capture, edit, and generate images to communicate: photos, AI-generated pictures, and polished sets for real audiences.

## Depth levels

**1 — Introductory.** *(AI draft — review)* Using an iPad or tablet, take specific types of photos on request — a close-up, a wide shot, a photo of your build, a photo of a person who says yes first. Hold still, check your shot, and retake until it shows what you meant.

**2 — Early.** *(AI draft — review)* Edit photos for a project: crop for framing, adjust light and color, and save the edited copy into the right folder with a clear name.

**3 — Preparatory.** *(AI draft — review)* Using Adobe Express or a similar tool, generate images with AI: write a prompt, look critically at what comes back, and refine or regenerate until it fits your project. Edit the result, and always say which images are AI-made.

**4 — Intermediate.** *(AI draft — review)* Combine, retouch, or generate images (remove a distraction, add text or stickers, generate a missing element) for a poster or post — choosing hand-editing or AI for each step, whichever serves the goal — keeping the original file safe.

**5 — Advanced.** *(AI draft — review)* Plan and produce a set of images with a consistent look for a real audience (exhibition, blog post) using any mix of photography, editing, and AI generation — and explain each choice of tool and why.
