# Work Plan

Plan your project: from design thinking boards to full project proposals.

## Depth levels

**1 — Introductory.** As a class, fill out a design stages board (get inspired, empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test, share) with your templates and project pictures.

**2 — Early.** Organize your own templates and project pictures into a Design Stages notebook, so you can see your project's journey stage by stage.

**3 — Preparatory.** Organize your design stages notebook and track a summary of your progress on a template: what is done, what is next, what is blocked.

**4 — Intermediate.** Create a design stages wall board and track your project on it, moving items along as work moves through the stages.

**5 — Advanced.** Create a design stages board in both physical and electronic form, and keep them updated as your project moves through all seven stages.

## Additional track: Business Plan

**1 — Introductory.** *Not introduced at this level.*

**2 — Early.** *Not introduced at this level.*

**3 — Preparatory.** Write what your project needs — time, skills, resources — using a simple classroom template.

**4 — Intermediate.** Write a project proposal covering time, skills, and resources, using a classroom template. Present it for approval before you start building.

**5 — Advanced.** Write a project proposal using a professional template that you find and the teacher approves. Include a materials list or budget with real numbers.
