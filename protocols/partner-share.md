## In a sentence

Each maker shows their work to one partner and listens to theirs in return.

## Why use it

The fastest, lowest-stakes way to make sure every maker presents out loud.
Ideal when time is short, when the room is too wound up for a whole-class
format, or as a warm-up before a bigger share.

## Steps

1. Pair up makers (elbow partners, or draw sticks for new pairs).
2. Partner A shows their work and says one sentence about it; Partner B listens,
   then says one thing they noticed and asks one question.
3. Swap roles.
4. Optional: a few pairs share one thing their partner made or said to the whole
   class.

## Teacher moves

- Give the listener a job ("notice one thing, ask one question") so listening
  stays active.
- Time each half so both partners get equal air.

## Make it smaller / bigger

*Smaller:* just the show-and-one-sentence, no question, for the youngest makers.
*Bigger:* rotate partners twice so each maker presents to three people and
refines their sentence each time.
