## In a sentence

All work is displayed like a museum; makers walk the room quietly and leave
feedback, then return to their own piece.

## Why use it

The quiet counterpart to the Carousel Walk — good when you want calm focus, or
when work is flat (drawings, worksheets, prototypes) and doesn't need a live
demo. Doubles as a Test-phase feedback method, not just a Share.

## Steps

1. Lay all work out with a feedback slip or sticky beside each.
2. Makers walk the room silently, viewing every piece.
3. Each maker leaves feedback at two or three pieces (a kind note, a wondering,
   or a star on the part that works).
4. Makers return to their own work and read what was left.

## Teacher moves

- Set the norm before the walk: feedback is about the work, useful and kind.
- Model a weak note ("cool!") versus a strong one ("the folded base makes it
  sturdy") so slips are worth reading.

## Make it smaller / bigger

*Smaller:* walk in two shifts (half display, half walk) so it's less crowded.
*Bigger:* add a sentence-starter menu on the slip, and give makers time to act
on one piece of feedback afterward.
